About

I am a senior at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University pursuing a B.S. in Astronomy and Astrophysics with a minor in Applied Mathematics. My research focuses on understanding the universe through observational and computational approaches, with particular interests in cosmology, galaxy evolution, and stellar dynamics.

Research Experience

Coincidental Events and Signal Reflection/Refraction at RNO-G Stations (University of Kansas)

Advisor: Dr. David Z. Besson | Timeline: May 2026 – Present

Analyzing coincidental radio-signal events across multiple RNO-G stations and analyzing real and simulated refracted and direct waveforms at varying ice depths. Utilized cross-station events to identify coincidental signals found at more than 2 stations. Computed refracted and direct signal amplitude ratios and time delays to compare real calibration data to simulation. Invited to continue as a remote research assistant during the academic year.

Observational Astronomy: Messier 51 Hα Emission (ERAU)

Advisor: Dr. Ted von Hippel | Timeline: January 2026 – April 2026

Conducted observational astronomy observation of Messier 51 using the ERAU 1-meter telescope, collecting 60+ hours of imaging data. Performed image reduction including bias subtraction, flat-field correction, and dark-frame correction using AstroImageJ. Analyzed Hα emission from H II regions to measure radial trends in relative intensities across the galaxy. Generated profiles and measurements of emission distribution patterns.

Pellets at Columbia (PAC) Test Stand Diagnostics (Columbia University)

Advisor: Dr. Carlos Paz-Soldan | Timeline: June 2025 – Present

Designed and operated diagnostic systems for the PAC fusion technology test stand, including camera, microwave cavity systems, and pellet tracking. Developed Python scripts to calculate cryogenic ice pellet masses from oscilloscope data. Independently operated and maintained the test stand, diagnosing and repairing vacuum leaks, hardware malfunctions, and electronic circuitry. Analyzed pellet deflection angles to prepare for e-beam ablation experiments.

Galaxy Structure Analysis with JWST (ERAU)

Advisor: Dr. Bililign Dullo | Timeline: April 2025 – Present

Conducting photometric and structural analysis of early-type galaxies using JWST data. Generated one- and two-dimensional brightness profiles, model residuals, and parameter fits for NGC 4406 using Photutils and Imfit software.

Stellar Gyrochronology with TESS (ERAU)

Advisor: Dr. Terry D. Oswalt | Timeline: August 2023 – Present

Developed Python algorithms using synchrosqueezed wavelet methods to analyze TESS light curves and identify rotation frequencies in wide-binary stars. Investigated relationships between stellar activity, rotation period, and age.
